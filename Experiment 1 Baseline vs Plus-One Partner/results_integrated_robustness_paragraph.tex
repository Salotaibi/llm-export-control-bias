\paragraph{Robustness (integrated).}
On a 200-pair pilot at $T=0.2$, overall agreement with deterministic runs ($T=0.0$) was 93.49\% (per model: ChatGPT-4o 93.49\%, Claude 98.14\%, Gemini 95.35\%, LLaMA 95.81\%, Qwen 84.65\%).
However, rank robustness was limited, with only 14 overlapping countries (e.g., ChatGPT-4o $\rho \approx 0.73$; LLaMA $\rho \approx 0.00$).
To verify these preliminary results, we conducted a targeted 500-pair check across 15 projects and 106 countries.
Overall label agreement at $T=0.2$ was 93.71\% (per model: ChatGPT-4o 95.53\%, Claude 98.64\%, Gemini 93.01\%, LLaMA 94.37\%, Qwen 86.99\%).
Country-level $\Delta$ rankings were moderately stable for ChatGPT-4o ($\rho = 0.59$) and Claude ($\rho = 0.70$), but weaker for Gemini, LLaMA, and Qwen ($\rho \approx 0.23$--$0.28$), indicating non-uniform stability across models.
We therefore retain the main estimates at $T=0.0$ and flag Qwen and, to a lesser extent, Gemini/LLaMA as fragile under mild sampling variation.
Models or countries failing robustness thresholds ($\ge 95\%$ agreement; $\rho \ge 0.9$) were interpreted with caution.
